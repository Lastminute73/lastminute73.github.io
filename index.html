1. Baseball ‚öæ

A 7-Card Stud game with specific wild cards.

How to Play

Each player is dealt 7 cards (2 down, 4 up, 1 down), with a round of betting after each face-up card is dealt. The goal is to make the best 5-card poker hand.

Special Rules

  • 3s and 9s are Wild: Any 3 or 9 can be used as any card.
  • 4s Get an Extra Card: If you are dealt a 4 face-up, you must immediately pay a set amount (e.g., a dime) to the pot to receive one extra card dealt face down.

2. The Cross ÂçÅÂ≠ó

A community card game where you use a shared cross of cards.

How to Play

  1. Each player is dealt four cards face down, followed by a round of betting.
  2. The dealer lays out five community cards face down in the shape of a cross.
  3. The dealer reveals the cards line by line (horizontal or vertical), with a betting round after each reveal.
  4. Players must make the best 5-card hand using two cards from their hand combined with the three cards from either the horizontal line or the vertical line of the cross.

3. Follow the Queen üëë

A 7-Card Stud game with a wild card that can change at any moment.

How to Play

Played exactly like standard 7-Card Stud.

Special Rules

  • All Queens are wild.
  • The card dealt face-up immediately following a Queen is also wild for all players.
  • If another Queen is dealt face-up later in the hand, the wild card designation shifts to whatever new card follows it.

A 7-Card Stud game with a "poison" card that resets the hand.

How to Play

Played exactly like standard 7-Card Stud.

Special Rule

  • If the Queen of Spades (Q‚ô†) is dealt to any player face-up, the hand is immediately dead.
  • All cards are discarded, everyone re-antes, and the pot from the dead hand is carried over to the new deal, creating a larger pot.

5. Pass the Trash ‚ôªÔ∏è

A 7-card game where you try to build the best hand while passing your unwanted cards.

How to Play

  1. Each player is dealt seven cards.
  2. Players select three cards to pass to the player on their left. A round of betting follows.
  3. This is typically followed by passing two cards, betting, then passing one final card, followed by a final betting round.
  4. The player with the best 5-card poker hand wins.

6. Anaconda üêç

A fast and simple game of nerve where losers pay big.

How to Play

  1. Each player is dealt two cards face down.
  2. Simultaneously, all players declare if they are "in" or "out" for the hand.
  3. The players who are "in" reveal their hands. The best high hand (e.g., a pair) wins the pot.

The Catch: All players who stayed "in" but lost must match the amount that was in the pot, creating a huge pot for the next hand.

7. Acey-Deucey ‚ô†Ô∏èACE DEUCE‚ô¶Ô∏è

A game of chance played between each player and the pot.

How to Play

  1. The pot is started with an ante from each player.
  2. The dealer deals two cards face-up to the player on their left.
  3. That player can bet any amount (from the minimum bet up to the total value of the pot) that the next card dealt will fall between the values of their two cards.
  4. Win: The player takes their wager from the pot.
  5. Lose: The player pays their wager into the pot. (Hitting one of the card values is a loss).
  6. Play moves to the next person.

8. 7-Card Stud High Diamond ‚ô¶Ô∏è

A split-pot game with a unique twist and a penalty for losers.

How to Play

Played as a standard game of 7-Card Stud. At the showdown, the pot is split into two equal halves.

Winning the Pot

  • Half the pot goes to the player with the best 5-card poker hand.
  • The other half goes to the player holding the single highest-ranking Diamond in their 7-card hand (Ace is high).

The Penalty: Any player at the showdown who does not win either half of the pot must match the entire pot. This pot is then carried over to the next hand.

9. Chase the Ace üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏èüí®

A one-card elimination game where the goal is to not be last.

Objective & Setup

To avoid having the lowest card and be the last player with "lives" remaining. Each player starts with three quarters in front of them as their "lives."

How to Play

  1. The dealer gives one card to each player. Kings are "safe" and are turned face-up immediately.
  2. Starting to the dealer's left, each player can either keep their card or trade it with the person to their left (who must accept, unless they have a King).
  3. The dealer can trade their card for the top card of the deck.
  4. Everyone reveals their card. The player with the lowest card (Ace is low) puts one of their quarters in the pot.
  5. The last player with quarters left wins the entire pot.
