1. Baseball ‚öæ

A 7-Card Stud variant with wild cards and special rules.

Game Play

The game follows the structure of 7-Card Stud. Each player receives two cards face-down and one card face-up, followed by a betting round. Three more cards are dealt face-up one at a time, with a betting round after each. The final card is dealt face-down, followed by a final betting round. Players then form their best 5-card hand.

Special Rules

  • Wild Cards: All 3s and 9s are wild.
  • Buy a Card: If a player is dealt a 4 face-up, they must pay a fee to the pot to receive an additional card face-down.

2. The Cross ÂçÅÂ≠ó

A community card game using a cross-shaped board.

Game Play

  1. Each player is dealt four private cards face-down (hole cards). A round of betting occurs.
  2. The dealer places five community cards face-down in a cross formation (one center, four arms).
  3. The cards are revealed one section at a time (e.g., the horizontal row, then the vertical row), with a betting round after each reveal.
  4. To make their hand, players must use exactly **two** of their hole cards with the three cards forming **either** the horizontal or vertical line of the cross.

3. Follow the Queen üëë

A 7-Card Stud variant with a dynamic wild card.

Game Play

This game is played identically to standard 7-Card Stud.

Wild Card Rules

  • All Queens are always wild.
  • The next card dealt face-up immediately after a Queen also becomes wild for all players.
  • If a subsequent Queen is dealt face-up, the previous "follower" card is no longer wild, and this new Queen's follower becomes the new wild card.

A special condition applied to 7-Card Stud that can void the hand.

Game Play

Played as a standard game of 7-Card Stud.

The Queen of Spades Rule

  • If the **Queen of Spades (Q‚ô†)** is dealt face-up to any player at any time, the hand is immediately declared dead.
  • All cards are collected, the pot remains, everyone must ante again, and a new hand is dealt by the same dealer.

5. Pass the Trash ‚ôªÔ∏è

A game where players pass cards to improve their hand.

Game Play

  1. Each player is dealt a hand of seven cards.
  2. Players choose three cards to pass to the player on their left. A round of betting occurs after everyone has received their new cards.
  3. Typically, this is followed by a round of passing two cards, another betting round, and a final round of passing one card and betting.
  4. After all passes, players form their best 5-card poker hand to determine the winner.

6. Anaconda üêç

A simple game of nerve where losing can be costly.

Game Play

  1. Each player is dealt two cards face-down.
  2. On a count of three, all players who wish to compete for the pot simultaneously declare they are "in" (often by holding their cards out). Players who fold pull their cards back.
  3. The players who remained "in" reveal their hands. The best high hand wins the pot.

The Catch: Any player who stayed in the hand but did not win must pay a penalty to the next pot equal to the full amount of the pot that was just won.

7. Acey-Deucey ‚ô†Ô∏èACE DEUCE‚ô¶Ô∏è

A betting game where players wager against the pot.

Game Play

  1. Each player contributes an ante to start the pot.
  2. In turn, starting to the dealer's left, each player is dealt two cards face-up.
  3. The player may bet any amount, up to the current total of the pot, that the third card dealt will have a rank that falls between the first two.
  4. **Winning Bet:** The player collects their wager from the pot.
  5. **Losing Bet:** The player must pay their wager into the pot. A bet is lost if the third card is outside the range or matches the rank of either of the first two cards ("hitting the post").

8. 7-Card Stud High Diamond ‚ô¶Ô∏è

A split-pot variant of 7-Card Stud with a penalty for non-winners.

Game Play

Played as a standard game of 7-Card Stud. At the showdown, the pot is divided equally between two winners.

Winning Conditions

  • High Hand: One half of the pot is awarded to the player with the best traditional 5-card poker hand.
  • High Diamond: The other half is awarded to the player who holds the single highest-ranking card of the Diamond suit among their seven cards (Ace of Diamonds is the highest possible).

The Penalty: At the showdown, any player who does not win either the High Hand or the High Diamond must match the entire pot, which carries over to the next hand.

9. Chase the Ace üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏èüí®

An elimination game where the goal is simply to not have the lowest card.

Objective & Setup

Be the last player with "lives" (quarters) remaining. Each player begins with three quarters placed in front of them.

Game Play

  1. One card is dealt to each player. A player dealt a King is safe for the round and turns it face-up.
  2. Beginning with the player to the dealer's left, each player must either keep their card or trade it with the player to their left. The trade is mandatory for the receiving player unless they hold a King.
  3. The dealer has the final option, and may either keep their card or exchange it for the top card of the deck.
  4. All cards are revealed. The player with the lowest ranking card (Ace is low) loses a "life" and must place one quarter in the pot. The last player with quarters wins the pot.
