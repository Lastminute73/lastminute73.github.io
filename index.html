Building Grave Digger

This section covers the transformation of your black Joyraver 24v into a monster icon. The focus is on the signature flames, graveyard mural, and spooky details.

Inspiration Gallery

The iconic Grave Digger.

A toy model showing the mural and flame details.

1. The Base: Perfect Start!

Your black Joyraver 24v is the ideal base. Just give it a thorough cleaning with soap and water, then wipe with isopropyl alcohol on areas you plan to paint or apply decals to.

2. The Green Flames

Create flame stencils using painter's tape and paper. Mask off the areas around the front and sides. Apply a plastic-safe primer, then spray with a vibrant lime green paint designed for plastic.

3. The Graveyard Mural

This is the signature detail. The easiest method is to find a high-resolution version of the mural online and have it printed on large adhesive vinyl sheets. Carefully apply it to the side panels of the truck.

4. Purple Chassis

To get the authentic look, paint the suspension components and parts of the undercarriage purple. Use painter's tape and plastic sheeting to protect the black body from overspray.

5. Spooky Details

Use printable vinyl for the "Grave Digger" logo on the door and the "Bad to the Bone" slogan. Paint the headlight inserts silver, then cover them with red translucent vinyl film for the iconic glowing red eyes.

6. Finishing Touches

Create flags using the Grave Digger skull logo printed on fabric. Attach them to small dowel rods and mount them to the back of the truck for the final arena-ready look.

Accessorizing the Pit Crew

You've got the jumpsuits handled, which is awesome! This section is all about the accessories that will complete your family's professional pit crew look.

Parents' Crew Gear

  • Tool Belts: Inexpensive toy tool belts add a great touch of authenticity.
  • Hats & Headsets: Simple black baseball caps and toy headsets or walkie-talkies.
  • Finishing Touches: Use black face paint or makeup for realistic "grease" smudges on your faces and hands. Carry checkered flags or stop/go paddles.

Baby Crew Chief

  • Outfit: A comfortable black onesie and a matching black beanie.
  • Custom Touch: Create a simple iron-on decal of a green skull or the word "CHIEF" for the onesie.
  • Accessories: A pair of baby-sized noise-canceling headphones (for the look) and a soft, plush toy wrench or tire.

Attitude & Fun

  • Get into character! Act busy, check the tires, and communicate on your walkie-talkies.
  • The pit crew's job is to make the driver look good, so cheer your son on as he drives his Grave Digger truck.
  • Have fun with it! The coordinated family effort is what makes this costume special.
